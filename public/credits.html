Credits and context

This is a living memorial by Chad Thompson-Smith for his mother, Cathy. Memory is presented as memory. Historical and economic claims are linked to their sources.

Historical context

  • Artie Romero's Colorado Springs video-game history documents the 1986 Nickels & Dimes Saturday parties at 710 E. Fillmore ($2.50 for two hours), the 1987 Boardwalk opening, and its eventual $5 all-you-can-play admission. The surviving copyrighted photographs are linked at their source and are not rehosted here.
  • U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics historical CPI-U table supplies the 1987 annual index and June 2026 index used by the token ledger.

Artwork and trademarks

The people-free arcade environment is AI-assisted original artwork. It does not reproduce family photographs, commercial game characters, or cabinet art. Mentioned game names belong to their respective owners and are included only as personal historical memories.

Skyline Smash, Token Trail, Dungeon Circuit, Highrise Havoc, Sunset Run, and Dragonfire Descent are original browser games created for this memorial. Their procedural graphics, mechanics, names, enemies, environments, levels, and cabinet presentation do not use third-party game assets.

The photo-booth portraits and celebration-of-life program are family-owned materials supplied and authorized for this memorial by Chad Thompson-Smith. The web copies preserve the real photograph, remove private file metadata, and use only deterministic resizing, compression, and CSS cropping. No face or likeness was generated, replaced, or synthesized.

Biographical details about Catherine Denise Smith-Fitts are drawn from the family-authorized program. The public narrative intentionally summarizes her life without reproducing the full list of living relatives.

Memorial photographs and family materials remain the property of the family and are not covered by the source-code license.

Sound and privacy

The optional soundtrack and game effects are generated in the browser with the Web Audio API. "Fillmore Drive" and "Moxie's Midnight Run" are original procedural compositions. "Mountain King '86" is a new browser arrangement of Edvard Grieg's public-domain composition "In the Hall of the Mountain King." No third-party recording is used, streamed, or stored.

High scores stay in browser-local storage. The site uses no analytics, advertising, cookies, accounts, or tracking pixels.
